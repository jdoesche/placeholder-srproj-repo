Purpose

Starting for the 2024-2025 school year, Tennessee mandated new K-12 Computer Science (CS) standards across all grade levels. Although there is a publicly available guidebook for these standards, the layout appears overwhelming, complex, and difficult for parents to interpret. Not only are an estimated 60% of adults in Tennessee reading at or below an 8th grade level, but most parents’ own education has little to no experience with CS. This inexperience can cause anxiety, lack of confidence, and gendered assumptions about technology which can directly affect parent involvement. Research suggests that not only is parent involvement critical for positive outcomes for a child’s education, but that there should be more STEM resources/support for parents by using technology.
